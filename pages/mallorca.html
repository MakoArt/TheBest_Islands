MALLORCA ISLANDS

Mallorca is a Spanish island located in the central part of the Balearic archipelago,in the Mediterranean Sea. Its capital, and also that of the autonomous community of the Balearic Islands, is Palma de Mallorca,1following it in importance the municipalities of Calviá, Inca and Manacor. The island of Cabrera and all its islets belong administratively to the capital. Mallorca has 3640.11 km2 (square kilometers), making it the largest in Spain and the seventh largest in the Mediterranean. their 859,289 inhabitants2(INE 2015) makes it the most populous island in the Balearic archipelago and the second most populous island in Spain after Tenerife.3​4In addition, it is also the fourth most populous island in the Mediterranean Sea.5Mallorca has a population density of 240.45 inhabitants/km2 (inhabitants per square kilometer). In the past, for its quiet atmosphere, it was also known as "The Island of Calm", but now it is a very important tourist destination, both nationally and internationally.6Mallorca has 550 km (kilometers) of coastline with more than 345 beaches of a wide variety of types, from small stone coves to long sandy beaches.7​
